My Journey

Fall 2019 – Started College at UMD

I started my college education at the University of Maryland pursuing a B.S. in Government and Politics. As I attended college, I had the opportunity to gain some professional experience as a student.

2021 – Student Ambassador for UMD SPP

During my Sophomore year, I was a Student Ambassador for the School of Public Policy. I was tasked with Marketing, hosting student engagement events, and facilitating recruitment through social media platforms.

2022 – Legislative Intern at MGA

Was a Legislative Intern for Delegate Alonzo T. Washington (District 22) at the Maryland General Assembly.

2022 – Speaker - UMD Art History Symposium

Presented my research paper, "Avant-Garde Under Radicalism: Nazism and Art" at the annual Undergraduate Art History Symposium.

2022 – White House Internship

Correspondence Intern working for the Office of the Vice President. Biden-Harris Administration.

2022-23 – University Teaching Assistant

Aided Professor Johanna Birnir and Professor Sarah Croco in GVPT201 Scopes and Methods for Political Science Research as a UTA, leading discussion sections and teaching statistical analysis through R programming language.

2023 – Data Curation Fellow

Part of Inaugural Cohort of Data Curation Fellows at the University of Maryland School of Behavioral and Social Sciences creating open data sets for social science instruction for research.

2023 – Government Relations Intern

Worked as a Government Relations Intern at the Electronic Transactions Association, researching legislation relating to transactions industry and emerging financial markets.

2023 – DC DataFest Winner

Participated in a weekend long data science competition, hosted by Summit Consulting LLC, where teams compete and use data to provide informative business insights for this year's client (The American Bar Association). Received 2/3 awards for Best Insight and Best Visualization.

Spring 2023 – Graduated College

Graduated College at the University of Maryland, College Park with a B.S. in Government and Politics (Honors Program)

2023-24 – Research and Technology Assistant

Helped develop data infrastructure of the Civics Center Organization, as well as expand the scope of research and automate data collection processes.

2023 - Present – Joined US HUD

Started working at the US Department of Housing and Urban Development as a Student Fellow, under the Pathways Program. In a little more than a year, I decided to accept a full-time position, working as a Data Analyst for the Office of Compliance and Disability Rights. As one of the sole Data Professionals in my office. I was in charge of providing technical support through data analysis in Fair Housing and Lending investigations, build concise data visualizations as well as maps, create new internal tools that streamline workflows and increase data accessibility for field colleagues, and research and provided insight into regulations and rule-making regarding Fair Housing.

Spring 2024 - Present – MS at GaTech

Enrolled in Masters of Science in Analytics at the Georgia Institute of Technology.

April 2025 – Temporary Termination from HUD

Following Executive Order 14284—Strengthening Probationary Periods in the Federal Service, I was illegally terminated from my position as a Data Analyst at HUD. Not a week later, my position was reinstated as I obtained an exception from the probationary period requirement due to my service as a Pathways Fellow.

April 2025 – Deferred Resignation Program

On April 11th, I accepted the Deferred Resignation Program 2.0, offered by the Department of Housing and Urban Development. In turn, I will be placed on Administrative Leave from my position until the end of September.

Present

Currently exploring new opportunities where I can leverage my combined expertise in data science and web development to create impactful solutions.
